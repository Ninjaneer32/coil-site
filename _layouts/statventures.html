Supply Chain Challenge

###MOBILE#### Data is greater than the sum of its parts.

Help us identify & use new ways to produce more timely, granular, and robust information about our nation’s supply chain infrastructure.

In order to support the nation’s need for information on critical goods, the U.S. Census Bureau seeks to improve its capability to measure the flow of goods through the supply chain. We primarily collect information on the domestic movement of goods through surveys and shipment records provided by businesses across America. We’re seeking new ways to improve or replace current data sources and methodologies.

Supply Chain Challenge

Data is greater than the sum of its parts.

Help us identify & use new ways to produce more timely, granular, and robust information about our nation's supply chain infrastructure.

In order to support the nation’s need for information on critical goods, the U.S. Census Bureau seeks to improve its capability to measure the end-to-end flow of goods through the supply chain. We primarily collect information on the

domestic movement of goods through surveys and shipment records provided by businesses across America. We’re seeking new ways to improve or replace current data sources and methodologies.

StatVentures challenges help us iteratively test and fund cutting-edge technologies and ideas, while providing direct partnership and feedback from the Census Bureau to make government adoption of new approaches faster and easier.

Phase 1: Concept

Propose an idea to improve U.S. Census data sources. 10 of the most innovative and promising ideas will be awarded $10,000 and their submitters will be invited to join the inaugural StatVentures cohort.

Phase 2: Roadmap

Iterate on your idea from phase 1, with direct collaboration and feedback from the U.S. Census Bureau team. Finalists with the most promising roadmaps will receive awards to move from plan to prototype. Missed phase 1? Don’t worry, new “walk on” teams have the opportunity to join in phases 2 and 3.

Phase 3: Test

Build your prototype and test it in real-world scenarios. Prototype demos are evaluated on their potential to serve the U.S. Census Bureau’s mission and goals, and ultimately have the opportunity to be awarded additional funds and pursue adoption by the U.S. Census Bureau.
